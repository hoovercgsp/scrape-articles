JERUSALEM, Jan. 29 (Xinhua) -- The Israeli Ministry of Economy and Industry on Wednesday announced the launch of a nationwide plan to establish infrastructure across hi-tech and deep-tech sectors.
According to a ministry statement, the initiative includes 13 selected projects with an investment of 220 million shekels (about 61 million U.S. dollars).
The initiative aims to create a robust infrastructure to support the growth of emerging companies, generate high-quality jobs, boost Israeli exports, and solidify Israel's position as a global leader in entrepreneurship and innovation, said the statement.
