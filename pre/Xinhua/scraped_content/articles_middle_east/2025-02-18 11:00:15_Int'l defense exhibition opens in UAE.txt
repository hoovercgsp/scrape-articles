ABU DHABI, Feb. 17 (Xinhua) -- The 2025 International Defense Exhibition and Conference, alongside the Naval Defense and Maritime Security Exhibition, opened here on Monday in the capital of the United Arab Emirates (UAE).
The event attracted 1,565 defense companies from 65 countries to showcase the latest defense technologies and solutions aimed at promoting international peace and security.
This year's exhibition space has expanded by 10 percent, drawing seven new countries and 731 new companies.
Leading Chinese defense companies are also participating in the event to exhibit a range of naval equipment, including surface vessels, submarines, underwater weapons and unmanned systems, as well as products from other defense sectors such as land, air and space.
The display of its latest advancements in naval equipment research, design, construction and integration by China Shipbuilding Group Corporation -- one of the world's largest shipbuilding groups -- underscores China's important role in the global defense sector.
UAE President Sheikh Mohamed bin Zayed Al Nahyan visited the exhibition, noting that it has become a critical platform for the global defense industry, which attracts top-tier enterprises and experts worldwide to drive progress in global security and stability.
